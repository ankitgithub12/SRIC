Admission Process

Join SRIC Senior Secondary School through our simple and transparent admission procedure

Apply Now View Fee Structure

Our Admission Journey

1

Inquiry & Registration

Begin by submitting an inquiry form online or visiting our campus. Our admission counselors will guide you through the process and help you register for admission.

2

Submission of Documents

Submit required documents including previous academic records, transfer certificate (if applicable), and identity proof. See complete list below.

3

Interaction Session

Students and parents meet with our academic team to discuss educational goals, expectations, and assess the student's readiness for our curriculum.

4

Admission Decision

Within 7 working days of document submission and interaction, you'll receive an admission decision via email and phone.

5

Fee Payment

Upon acceptance, complete the admission by paying the required fees within the stipulated time to secure your seat.

6

Orientation

Attend the orientation program before the academic session begins to familiarize yourself with school policies, faculty, and facilities.

Required Documents

Academic Records

  • Report card of previous class
  • Board marksheet (for class 11/12)
  • Transfer Certificate (if applicable)

Identity Proof

  • Birth Certificate
  • Aadhaar Card (Student & Parents)
  • Passport size photos (4 copies)

Application Forms

  • Completed admission form
  • Medical history form
  • Transportation request (if needed)

Financial Documents

  • Fee payment receipt
  • Concession form (if applicable)
  • Scholarship documents

Frequently Asked Questions

The age criteria are as follows:

  • Class 9: Minimum 13 years as of March 31, 2025
  • Class 11: Minimum 15 years as of March 31, 2025
  • For other classes, the age should be appropriate for the grade level based on previous schooling

For classes 9 and 11, we conduct a basic aptitude test to assess the student's foundational knowledge. The test covers Mathematics, English, and General Science for Class 9, while for Class 11 it focuses on the subjects relevant to the chosen stream.

We maintain an optimal student-teacher ratio of 25:1 to ensure personalized attention for each student. In senior classes (11-12), the ratio is even better at 20:1 for core subjects.

Yes, we offer merit-based scholarships and need-based fee concessions. Merit scholarships are awarded based on previous academic performance (90%+ in board exams) and performance in our scholarship test. Need-based concessions require submission of income proof and are evaluated on a case-by-case basis.
